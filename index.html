Who are we?
A symbol of the history and legacy of the Pittsburgh city. Take a ride through history on the Duquesne Incline, a 19th-century funicular that’s been carrying passengers up Mount Washington since 1877. Originally built for workers, it’s now a must-visit spot for stunning skyline views and a glimpse into Pittsburgh’s past. Step aboard and see the city like never before!

Visitor's Guide

Why Visit Us?

Visiting the Duquesne Incline offers a unique blend of history, breathtaking views, and cultural significance. In operation since 1877, this historic cable car provides a glimpse into Pittsburgh’s industrial past while offering an unforgettable ride up Mount Washington. At the top, visitors enjoy stunning panoramic views of downtown, the city’s famous bridges, and the three rivers. The upper station museum showcases original 19th-century machinery and historic photos. Whether you’re a history buff, photographer, or just looking for a unique experience, the Duquesne Incline is a must-visit. Plus, it’s a great starting point for exploring Mount Washington’s restaurants and scenic overlooks.
